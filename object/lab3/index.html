PART 1: Soldering Setup & Safety

Soldering Station

Here are all of the tools I used at the soldering station.

Soldering Safety

  • Temperature: Keep the iron between 600°F – 700°F.
  • Handling: Hold the iron by the handle only; never touch the metal parts.
  • Storage: Always return the iron to its stand when not in use.
  • Fumes: Turn on the fume extractor to avoid inhaling harmful smoke.
  • Protection: Wear safety glasses to protect your eyes from splatters.
  • Hygiene: No eating or drinking nearby, and wash your hands with soap when finished.

Wire Solder Joints

Here, I tried to solder the wires without using too much solder.

"Which type of wire (stranded or solid-core) did you find easier to solder to the protoboard?"

The solid-core wire was easier to solder to the protoboard because it was easier to keep still, and it was easier to mostly cover in solder. I think that was also partially because I stripped a little more off of the end of the stranded wire than the solid-core wire, so the stranded wire had more exposed wire that I had to try to cover with solder.


PART 3: Bridge Of Solder

Bridges

I found this to be a bit difficult because the solder frequently wouldn't stay in the shape I wanted it to stay in. The instructions in the lab description to put the solder on adjacent pins before completing the bridge was super helpful.

Part 4: Splicing Wires

Spliced Wires

I put the wires upright, put a little bit of solder on each side (that I'm going to connect together), then I heated up the solder once I arranged the wires so that they would create a longer wire.

Reflection

The last time I soldered was in middle school, so I had a lot of fun getting a better understanding about how the process works! Definitely was a bit of a learning curve, but ended up being very approachable.
